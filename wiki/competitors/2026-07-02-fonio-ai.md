# Competitive intel: fonio.ai vs Tilda (berlin-frontdesk-bot)

> Snapshot 2026-07-02. Chat session delivered the full comparison; this file is
> the durable record for diffing future runs. Prices fetched live from
> fonio.ai/en/pricing; scale/growth claims are company-reported PR.

## Who they are

Vienna, founded 2024. ~40 staff. **$17M seed at $140M valuation (Jun 2026,
led by 20VC)**; $20M+ raised total. Company-reported: 7,000+ customers, 2M+
automated calls/month, ~40% MoM growth. Active in 9 markets (DACH core; FR,
IT, PL, BR, UK, US new in 2026). OMR "#1 Software Tool 2026".

**Positioning:** "Der KI-Telefonassistent auf Deutsch." Voice-first AI phone
assistant, horizontal SMB/mid-market (medical, trades, law, tax, hotels,
automotive, property…), GDPR/EU-sovereignty as headline differentiator.
Claims fully proprietary stack (own STT/TTS/turn-detection/orchestration, no
US white-label), hosted in Nuremberg. EU AI Act compliance claimed.

## Product facts

- **Channels:** Voice core (inbound+outbound). **WhatsApp live as €79/mo
  add-on** (800 conversations, 1,000 contacts, 1 number). Email + web chat
  roadmapped ~Sept 2026. No conversational SMS.
- **Languages:** German primary/optimized; EN/FR/PL/IT/PT/ES supported;
  "25+ languages" claim unverified firsthand.
- **Booking:** historically via **Cal.com as intermediary** (Google/Outlook/
  iCloud through it); own "AI-native calendar" claimed live Jun 2026.
  AI-native CRM promised end Q3 2026. Reviewers flag native integrations as
  still limited (webhook/Zapier workarounds).
- **Config:** no-code, form/settings-based ("<10 min setup"); no visual flow
  builder (a top user request).
- **Telephony:** provisions own numbers (DACH strongest; extra number €7/mo).

## Strategic implications for Tilda

1. **PROJECT.md §4 is partially falsified.** "AI receptionist space is
   voice-first and US-centric… almost nobody is WhatsApp-first + German +
   GDPR" — fonio is German-first, GDPR-native, EU-hosted, well-funded, and
   already has WhatsApp shipped. The *US-centric* half of the gap thesis is
   dead; the *WhatsApp-first* half survives (WhatsApp is their bolt-on, not
   their wedge).
2. **They beat us on the data-sovereignty story.** Proprietary EU stack +
   Nuremberg hosting vs our Twilio + ElevenLabs + OpenRouter (US vendors with
   EU options). Against a compliance-savvy buyer (dental, F5) their pitch is
   stronger on paper; our mitigation is per-client sub-accounts, EU residency
   modes, DPAs — and not competing on infrastructure sovereignty at all.
3. **Price anchor pressure.** fonio Solo+WhatsApp = ~€178/mo self-serve vs
   our €250–400/mo WhatsApp tier + €500–1,500 setup. Our price only holds as
   **done-for-you service** (onboarding questionnaire, owner edits on
   request, parallel-run, monthly ROI proof), not as software.
4. **Defensible ground that survives:** WhatsApp-first for beauty (their
   voice-first model mismatches how salon customers actually reach out);
   niche depth vs their horizontal spread; real Google Calendar direct
   integration today vs their Cal.com detour; high-touch local Berlin
   service; owner-alert + one daily summary + business-memory/audit-log moat
   (F4). Their weaknesses map to our strengths: no trial (we do live demos),
   integrations immature, no flow control per business (our clients/*.yaml).
5. **Watch items:** their email/web-chat launch (~Sept 2026), AI-native CRM
   (end Q3 2026), whether WhatsApp moves from add-on to core, German-market
   pricing moves, and any beauty-vertical marketing.
